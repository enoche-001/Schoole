About Us

A Legacy of Excellence
Since the Year 2000

Established in the year 2000 and duly accredited by the relevant educational authorities, Brighton Heights School has maintained a distinguished record of academic attainment and moral formation across its primary and secondary programmes. The institution operates in strict adherence to the policies and guidelines of the Federal Ministry of Education and the Kogi State Ministry of Education, Science and Technology.

Our Mission

To deliver a structured, value-driven educational experience that equips every pupil with the intellectual capacity, ethical grounding, and personal discipline required to contribute meaningfully to national development and global progress.

Our Vision

To be recognised as the foremost institution of basic and post-basic education in Kogi State, producing graduates of unimpeachable character, sound academic preparation, and demonstrated civic responsibility.

Our Academic Programmes

Brighton Heights School offers a structured sequence of learning across three tiers of formal education, each designed in conformity with the approved national curriculum.

Primary School

Basic 1 – Basic 6

The primary programme provides the foundational framework upon which all subsequent learning is constructed. Instruction at this level develops functional literacy, numeracy, and scientific awareness in accordance with the approved 9-Year Basic Education Curriculum. Emphasis is placed on child-centred pedagogy, structured assessment, and the cultivation of responsible citizenship from an early stage.

English LanguageMathematics Basic Science & TechnologySocial Studies Cultural & Creative Arts

Junior Secondary School

JSS 1 – JSS 3

The Junior Secondary programme completes the Basic Education cycle and prepares students for the Basic Education Certificate Examination (BECE). The curriculum is broad-based, introducing pupils to a range of disciplines including the sciences, humanities, and vocational subjects, while reinforcing analytical reasoning and independent study skills essential for senior secondary progression.

MathematicsEnglish Language Integrated ScienceFrench Language Computer Studies

Senior Secondary School

SS 1 – SS 3

At the Senior Secondary level, students are enrolled in one of three approved elective clusters — Sciences, Humanities, or Commercial Studies — in preparation for the West African Senior School Certificate Examination (WASSCE), the National Examination Council (NECO) examinations, and the Unified Tertiary Matriculation Examination (UTME) conducted by JAMB. The school maintains dedicated examination preparation programmes and sustains a pass rate exceeding 98% at distinction level.

Admissions

Admission into Brighton Heights

The school operates a structured and transparent admissions procedure in compliance with the guidelines of the Kogi State Secondary Education Board. Prospective applicants are advised to follow the steps outlined below.

1
Submission of Application
Prospective candidates are required to complete the official online application form, providing accurate bio-data, the desired class of entry, and valid parent or guardian contact information. Applications must be submitted in full before consideration can be extended. No physical visit to the school premises is required at this stage of the process.
2
Payment of Application Fee
Upon submission of the application form, candidates are required to remit the prescribed non-refundable application processing fee via the designated online payment platform. Proof of payment in the form of an electronically generated receipt will be issued automatically and should be retained by the applicant for official reference purposes.
3
Submission of Required Documents
Applicants are to upload certified copies of all required credentials through the admissions portal. These include: a certified birth certificate or declaration of age, two recent passport photographs, the most recent terminal academic report, and — for candidates seeking admission into JSS 1 via transfer or SS 1 — a duly signed transfer certificate from the previous institution. Submitted documents shall be reviewed by the admissions committee within five working days of receipt.
4
Admission Decision
The outcome of the admissions review shall be communicated to applicants via SMS and electronic mail. Successful candidates will receive a formal offer of provisional admission accompanied by a unique student registration number. Where an offer is made subject to the confirmation of final academic results, such conditions shall be clearly stated in the admission letter.
5
Enrolment & Portal Activation
Following acceptance of the offer of admission, candidates are required to complete the payment of applicable school fees in order to secure their placement. Upon confirmation of payment, login credentials for the Brighton Heights student portal will be issued, granting access to the academic timetable, examination results, and official school communications. Resumption schedules and hostel allocation details will be conveyed at this stage.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

The following questions address matters commonly raised by prospective parents and guardians during the admissions process.

What is the school fees structure at Brighton Heights?
School fees are structured according to the level of study — Primary, Junior Secondary, or Senior Secondary — and are further differentiated by boarding arrangement. A detailed schedule of tuition, boarding, and sundry charges is enclosed with each offer of admission. Prospective parents seeking advance information on the current fee schedule are advised to contact the school's Bursary Department directly.
Is Brighton Heights a day school or a full boarding school?
Brighton Heights School operates principally as a full boarding institution for both primary and secondary pupils. The boarding structure is considered integral to the school's pastoral care model and its approach to academic oversight. Day student arrangements may be available for select classes subject to capacity and administrative approval — prospective parents are encouraged to direct such enquiries to the Admissions Office.
Does the school offer scholarships or fee concessions?
The school operates a merit-based scholarship programme open to academically distinguished candidates, as well as a limited number of concessions for verified cases of financial exigency. Scholarship applications are reviewed on an annual basis by a duly constituted committee. Interested applicants are requested to submit a formal letter of application, together with relevant supporting documentation, to the Office of the Principal.
What is the school's performance record in public examinations?
Brighton Heights School has consistently returned a pass rate in excess of 98% in both the West African Senior School Certificate Examination (WASSCE) and the National Examination Council (NECO) examinations, with notable distinctions recorded in Mathematics, English Language, Biology, and Economics. The school's SS3 programme incorporates a structured examination preparedness curriculum administered throughout the final academic session.
Does the school provide preparation for the UTME conducted by JAMB?
Yes. The school's Senior Secondary Three (SS3) programme incorporates structured preparatory classes for the Unified Tertiary Matriculation Examination (UTME), including simulated Computer-Based Test (CBT) sessions and subject-specific revision classes. Students also receive institutional guidance on JAMB registration procedures, choice of tertiary institution and course of study, and preparation for post-UTME screening exercises.
Can parents access their ward's academic records online?
Yes. Brighton Heights School operates a secure digital portal through which parents and legal guardians may access terminal examination results, academic progress reports, and official school communications pertaining to their ward. Login credentials are issued to guardians following the successful completion of the enrolment process.
What documents are required for the admissions process?
The following documents are required for all applications: a certified copy of the candidate's birth certificate or sworn declaration of age; two recent passport photographs with a white background; the candidate's most recent terminal academic report; and, in the case of transfer applicants into JSS 1 or SS 1, a duly authenticated transfer certificate from the previous school of attendance. All documents must be submitted as clear, legible digital copies through the online admissions portal.
What is the school's policy on mobile devices?
In accordance with the school's Student Conduct and Welfare Policy, the possession and use of personal mobile devices by boarding students is restricted during academic hours and supervised study periods. Supervised access may be permitted during designated periods as determined by the school administration. Full details of the mobile device policy are set out in the Student Handbook, which is issued to all students upon enrolment.
